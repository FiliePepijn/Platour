SoundLab.

Assignment.

For this assignment, we had to create branding for the Fontys SoundLab.
The stakeholder had a problem: the SoundLab was not well-known among students, and they wanted it to be used more. So, we created a brand and an awareness campaign.

Logo.

We started creating different logos. Eventually, we put all the logos in a survey and let the target audience make the decision. On the right, you see the logos I made. I made 3 logos and asked Josh which one he liked most; this one I put in the survey.

Style.Choices.

My first logo is a more modern/minimalistic logo because it has no doodles or other random elements. I made this modern minimalistic because many people like the modern vibe. Here you see the element above the text. It represents a sound wave, reconnecting to the sound because there you can make sound.

For this design, I went for a more playful vibe but still minimalistic. The lines at the top and by the exclamation mark make the logo more playful as well as the font; the letters are rotated. I also brought a sound wave into the 'o' from 'box' as lines.

This is more of a warm and cozy design. It is a retro font. The colors make it more cozy and warm. I made a line above the retro text to connect the logo to the audio lab. This line represents a sound wave. This is also the logo that was chosen by Josh, so I chose this one to put in the survey.

When we took the survey, we got 15 answers that said this is the most appealing logo. With this knowledge, we know that the target audience wanted a retro/cozy vibe. Here is a link to the survey answers: Survey

Poster.

After the logo was chosen, I made a poster for the SoundLab based on the retro/cozy vibe the target audience chose. Here you see the poster that I made. I used the logo and added the SoundLab text to make it clearer that it is for a SoundLab. I also gave some examples of what the SoundLab can be used for. The sheet music line makes the biggest connection with the SoundLab. The overall poster is like a photo from an instant camera, enhancing the retro feel of the poster.
